The Auditor's Job, Precisely
The compaction auditor's job is to distinguish:

Strongly lumpable: H¹(summary) = H¹(source), δ(summary) = δ(source), bond failure structure preserved — no flag
Weakly lumpable: H¹(summary) ≠ H¹(source) in general, δ may be wrong, EE profile of summary ≠ EE profile of source — flag
"Mostly lumpable": a weakly-lumpable compaction mislabeled as acceptable — the specific error the auditor should catch
Calling a weakly-lumpable compaction "mostly lumpable" does not move it closer to strongly lumpable. It is not a point on a dial. The categorical/conditional distinction is not gradations of the same thing — it is a qualitative difference in the kind of guarantee the summary provides. A "mostly lumpable" summary is lossy for some initial distributions, which means the auditor cannot know whether it is safe without checking against the source spans.
