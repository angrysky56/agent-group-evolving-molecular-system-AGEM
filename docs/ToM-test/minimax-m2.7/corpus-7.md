Epiphenomenalism vs interactionist dualism

These are opposite verdicts on the causal power of the mental, not variants of one view. Epiphenomenalism grants that mental states are real but denies they cause anything physical: they are causally inert by-products, like the steam whistle that accompanies but does not drive the train. Interactionist dualism (Descartes' descendants) insists the mental does cause the physical — decisions move bodies — accepting the burden of explaining how non-physical causes touch physical effects. One says the mind is a passenger; the other says it grips the wheel. A summary that lumps them as "dualism" erases the fact that they give contradictory answers to the single question dualism is most pressed on: whether minds do anything.
